# Instagram Outreach Playbook

This is the script and process for getting your first 5 paying stables. Founder-led, IG-first, no automation, no shortcuts. Read it once, then keep this file open in a tab while you send.

---

## Why Instagram (not email, not LinkedIn)

The equestrian world lives on Instagram. Stable owners post daily, check DMs hourly, and ignore most email. Response rate from a well-written IG DM in this space is **25–40%**. Cold email hits **<5%**. LinkedIn doesn't exist for these people.

Use a **personal account**, not a brand account. Brand accounts feel like marketing; personal feels like a human asking for help.

---

## Rules

- **Founder voice only.** You, not the brand.
- **Short.** If they need to scroll, it's too long.
- **No pitch in the opener.** Ask, don't sell.
- **Specific, not templated.** Reference one detail from their feed in every cold open.
- **Send Mon–Thu, 7–9am or 6–8pm local time.** Avoid weekends — stables are working.
- **Three follow-ups maximum, then stop.**
- **Track every send in your target sheet.** Every message, every reply, every visit.
- **No links in message #1.** Instagram deprioritises link-bearing first messages.

---

## Cold opens (pick one — don't mix in the same week)

### A — Founder-led, direct *(use this 80% of the time)*

> Hey [name] — I'm building a tool to help small stables track sessions and lessons, and stop losing client info to WhatsApp. You run [stable name] and I'd love 15 minutes of your time to show what we're building and ask whether it would actually help. Happy to come by — I'll bring coffee. No pitch, just curious.

### B — Compliment-based *(use when they post strong content)*

> Hey [name] — saw your reel about [specific thing they posted in last 30 days]. Quick ask: I'm building a tool for stables your size and I'd love your input for 15 minutes — what's actually broken in your day-to-day. Coffee on me. No pitch, I just want to learn.

### C — Question-led *(use when their feed is sparse)*

> Hey [name] — quick question. How do you currently track which horses got worked each day? Asking because I'm building something for stables and want to learn from people who actually run them. 15 min coffee if you're up for it.

## Follow-up cadence

### Follow-up 1 — 4 days after cold open, no reply
> [Name] — bumping this gently. If now's bad, I get it. I'll send one more nudge in a week and then disappear.

### Follow-up 2 — 10 days after cold open, still no reply
> Hey [name] — last try. We just shipped [feature / hit milestone — be specific]. If you'd ever want a 5-minute look I'd love to show you. If not, no worries — I'll see you around the [local fair / event].

### Follow-up 3 — soft yes, no booking yet
> Sounds great! Two options for next week: [Day] morning or [Day] late afternoon. I can come to the stable or jump on a 15-min video call. Whatever's easier for you.

---

## After the "yes"

1. **Confirm time and location within 1 hour.** Speed signals you take it seriously.
2. **Send a calendar invite** (Google or Apple — match what they use).
3. **Day-before reminder:** "Looking forward to tomorrow! Anything specific you'd like me to bring or show?"
4. **Visit prepared.** Bring a one-page printout of your wedge — they'll keep it on the office wall.
5. **Within 4 hours of the visit:** thank-you message + one specific thing you took away. Reference something they said. This is what turns a meeting into a relationship.

---

## After the "no"

> Totally understand. Mind if I check back in 6 months? Things might be different by then for both of us.

You'll be surprised how many "no"s become "yes"es 6 months later. Add them to the *nurture* status in your sheet. Send one quality update every 8 weeks — never a sales push.

---

## Conversion offer for the first 10 stables

When you sit down with them, lead with the "founding stable" deal:

- **50% off for life** (Starter €19/mo, Pro €39/mo)
- **Direct WhatsApp line to you**, the founder
- **Founding stable badge** in the product
- **Early access** to all V2 features

Frame: *"I'm only doing this for the first 10 stables. After that, full price."*

This is cheap insurance. They get a deal; you get social proof, testimonials, retained feedback, and ten people who feel ownership over your success.

---

## What NOT to send

- Mass-templated openers ("Hi! I noticed you run a stable...") — they smell terrible.
- Anything with the word "synergy."
- Long emails masquerading as DMs.
- Links in message #1.
- "Just checking in" — the most ignored phrase in B2B.
- Voice notes (in cold outreach — fine after they've replied).
- Pitch decks attached to a cold message.

## Daily rhythm (until you have 5 paying stables)

| Time | Action | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Mon 7–9am | Cold opens | 5 new stables |
| Tue 7–9am | Cold opens | 5 new stables |
| Wed 7–9am | Cold opens | 5 new stables |
| Thu 7–9am | Cold opens | 5 new stables |
| Daily 6–8pm | Follow-ups + replies | as needed |
| Fri morning | Update target sheet, plan next week | — |

**20 cold opens × 30% reply rate = 6 conversations per week.** With a ~25% conversion to a meeting and ~40% meeting-to-trial, you're at 1 trial per week. That's how you hit 5 paying stables in 8–10 weeks.

If your reply rate is below 15%, your message is wrong — change the opener, not the volume.

---

## Tracking — what to log every send

For each stable in your target sheet:

- Date of message
- Variant used (A / B / C)
- Reply received? (Y/N)
- Time to reply
- Outcome (no reply / reply / meeting / trial / customer / declined / nurture)
- Quote of best line they said back to you (use these on the landing page later)

Review the sheet every Friday. Patterns will emerge fast.
