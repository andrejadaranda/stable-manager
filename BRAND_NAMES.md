# Brand Name Shortlist

**Brief:** A B2B SaaS for stables that doubles as a B2C horse-activity layer for riders and owners. Premium feel. International. Should age into a category-defining brand. **Avoid** horse puns, anything ending in `-ly` / `-ify`, and anything that sounds like 2010 SaaS.

I've split into four directions, each with three names. After the table, my top 3 picks and a scoring framework so you can pressure-test.

---

## Direction A — Activity & rhythm
*The Strava/Cadence DNA. Makes the wedge — sessions, training rhythm — legible in the name.*

### 1. **Cadence**
- **Pron.** *KAY-dens*
- **Meaning:** the rhythm of a horse's gait; the beat in dressage and music.
- **Pros:** Premium-feeling. Equestrians already use the word daily. Suggests the activity layer without saying "horse." Travels in any market.
- **Cons:** Common word — hard to own .com (cadence.com is taken; cadence.app, cadence.co, getcadence.com may be options). Trademark crowded in B2B SaaS (Cadence Design Systems, sales tools).
- **Verdict:** Strongest position-fit name on this list, but the trademark and TLD work is heavy. Worth a real check.

### 2. **Stride**
- **Pron.** *strīd*
- **Meaning:** a unit of forward movement; in show jumping, the count between fences.
- **Pros:** Equestrian native, reads as confident, premium. Short. Memorable.
- **Cons:** Stride.com taken. Several SaaS already use it (Stride Health, etc.). Could work as `Stridehq.com` or with a country TLD.
- **Verdict:** Beautiful but probably not ownable globally. Good fallback or sub-brand.

### 3. **Tempo**
- **Pron.** *TEM-poh*
- **Meaning:** rhythm, pace.
- **Pros:** Soft, premium, internationally readable.
- **Cons:** Heavily used (Tempo time-tracking SaaS is the immediate collision). Generic.
- **Verdict:** Skip unless you can find an unclaimed angle.

## Direction B — Equestrian-rooted but not punny
*Insider words that sound like a brand, not a barn.*

### 4. **Hayloft**
- **Pron.** *HAY-loft*
- **Meaning:** the loft above a barn where hay is stored.
- **Pros:** Warm, ownable. Maps cleanly to "the place where information about your horse lives." Good metaphor for storage / archive of training history. Easy to brand visually.
- **Cons:** Slightly cute. May feel small as the company grows into V3 social features.
- **Verdict:** A serious contender. Feels like a real consumer product *and* a B2B tool.

### 5. **Paddock**
- **Pron.** *PAD-ock*
- **Meaning:** a small enclosed area where horses turn out.
- **Pros:** Universally recognised in the equestrian world. Ownable feel.
- **Cons:** Already a few small startups using it. F1 also owns the cultural mindshare ("the paddock"). Slightly retro.
- **Verdict:** Workable but not as clean as Hayloft.

### 6. **Cantle**
- **Pron.** *KAN-tul*
- **Meaning:** the raised back rim of a saddle.
- **Pros:** Insider term — equestrians smile when they hear it; outsiders don't know it, which makes it ownable. Short. Hard. Modern.
- **Cons:** Niche even within the niche. Marketing has to explain it for the first 6 months.
- **Verdict:** A bold pick. Strong if you commit; weak if you hedge.

## Direction C — Premium abstract
*Linear / Notion / Stripe energy. Names that don't telegraph the category — they earn it.*

### 7. **Stria**
- **Pron.** *STREE-uh*
- **Meaning:** Latin "furrow, line, stripe"; in anatomy, a band or streak.
- **Pros:** Premium, ownable, four letters of style. No equestrian baggage — works as an OS name.
- **Cons:** Means nothing to your customer until you put the meaning into it. Must invest in brand.
- **Verdict:** The "Stripe" play. High ceiling, higher cost.

### 8. **Verra**
- **Pron.** *VAIR-uh*
- **Meaning:** invented; evokes Latin *verus* (true) and Italian *vera*.
- **Pros:** Soft, premium, gender-neutral. Good for a multi-role product where trust matters (owners need to trust the data they see).
- **Cons:** Generic-feeling without a strong logo. Verra.com almost certainly taken; .co or .app likely.
- **Verdict:** Good utility name; weaker emotional hook than Hayloft or Cantle.

### 9. **Apex**
- **Pron.** *AY-peks*
- **Meaning:** highest point.
- **Pros:** Premium, aspirational, easy.
- **Cons:** Overused — Apex Legends, Apex CRM, Apex anything. No way to own it.
- **Verdict:** Skip. Listed only to mark off the obvious.

---

## Direction D — Coined / invented (most ownable)
*Made-up names you can trademark and bend to mean exactly what you want.*

### 10. **Equora**
- **Pron.** *eh-KWOR-uh*
- **Meaning:** coined from Latin *equus* (horse) + *ora* (now / hour / boundary).
- **Pros:** Clear category signal, but elevated. Premium feel. Likely available on most TLDs. Ownable trademark.
- **Cons:** "Equ-" prefix names sound like vet pharma (Equimax, Equaide). Risk of feeling clinical.
- **Verdict:** Solid. Test it on real trainers — does it feel premium or pharmaceutical?

### 11. **Stryde**
- **Pron.** *strīd*
- **Meaning:** alt spelling of "stride."
- **Pros:** Solves the Stride.com availability problem. Modern wordmark potential.
- **Cons:** Misspelt-word brands (Lyft, Tumblr) work when paired with strong design. They look cheap when they're not.
- **Verdict:** Decent backup if you love Stride but can't get it.

### 12. **Riden**
- **Pron.** *RYE-den*
- **Meaning:** coined; the past participle of "ride," with a distinct spelling.
- **Pros:** Verb-rooted, universally readable. Conjugates the activity ("you've ridden"). Ownable. Easy logo.
- **Cons:** Slightly soft. Could be confused with Roden, Riden Inc., misspellings of "ridden."
- **Verdict:** Strong, especially if you lean B2C eventually. Riden.com may be ownable; .co/.app definitely.

---

## Top 3 picks, in order

1. **Hayloft** — best balance of warmth, ownability, and category-fit. Works for every audience. Visual brand writes itself (warm wood, pale gold, archive aesthetic). My pick if I had to ship Monday.
2. **Cadence** — strongest position-fit name; sells itself. Only proceed if you can clear the trademark and grab a workable TLD (`getcadence.com`, `cadence.app`, `cadenceequine.com`).
3. **Riden** — if you want one foot in the B2C future. Verb-led, clean, ownable. Pair with a sharp wordmark and it can carry both sides of the platform.

**Honourable mention:** Cantle — a bolder, riskier choice. If you're confident in your brand work, this is the most ownable and most equestrian-feeling option here.

## Scoring framework — pressure-test before you pick

Score each name 1–5 across these six dimensions. Total of 24+ is shippable; 27+ is excellent.

| Dimension                   | What you're checking                                              |
|-----------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Memorable**               | Can a trainer remember it after one conversation?                 |
| **Pronounceable**           | Same name, same pronunciation across DE, FR, IT, EN, ES?          |
| **Spellable from sound**    | Can someone type it into a browser after hearing it once?         |
| **Position-fit**            | Does it suggest the wedge (activity / connection / horses)?       |
| **Ownable (legal)**         | Is the EU + US trademark available for SaaS class 9 / 42?         |
| **Ownable (digital)**       | Is .com or .eu + Instagram + LinkedIn handle available?           |

---

## Practical next steps (do this today)

1. **Pick your top 2.** Don't fall in love with one yet.
2. **TLD check** — namecheap.com or porkbun.com. Look for the .com first; settle for country-specific (.de, .at, .pl, .nl) only if .com is unobtainable.
3. **Trademark check** — [EUIPO eSearch](https://euipo.europa.eu/eSearch/) for the EU and [USPTO TESS](https://tmsearch.uspto.gov/) for the US. Class 9 (downloadable software) and Class 42 (SaaS).
4. **Instagram + TikTok handle check** — same handle on both, please.
5. **5-trainer gut check** — text your top 2 names to five real trainers in your network. "Quick one — which sounds more like a software you'd actually use? A or B?" Three-line reply, no context, no explanation. Trust the answer.
6. **Buy the domain that day.** Names die in indecision. €15 to lock it isn't a real cost.

## A quick word on what NOT to name it

- Any compound with "Equine," "Equi-," "Stable-," "Saddle-" → 90% of the category is already named this way; you'll disappear.
- Anything with "Pro," "App," "Hub," or "HQ" suffixed → reads as filler.
- Pegasus / Stallion / Unicorn / Mustang → cliché.
- "GiddyUp," "WhoaBoss," anything cute → kills B2B credibility on first contact.
- Three-syllable Latin compounds (Equestria, Stabula) → pretty in a logo, fatal in a sales call.
