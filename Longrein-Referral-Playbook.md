# Referral playbook — how to convert FM #1–#10 into stables #11–#30

**Each founding member committed to introducing 2 stables in their country within 6 months. That's 20 warm intros — the entire customer-acquisition engine for Q3'26. This file is how you make that real, not theoretical.**

> Voice rule on every send: written by Andreja in 1st person to the founding member. Never feels like a forwarded sales template.

---

## The asymmetry

20 warm intros at 30% close = 6 paying stables at month 13.
6 paying stables × €40/mo average × 24 months = €5,760 ARR with €0 acquisition cost.

Compare to cold outreach: 200 emails to land 1 paying stable. The ROI difference is 100×.

This is why the referral commitment is in the contract. This is why this playbook exists.

## Step 1 — The Month 3 ask (you've already sent it)

In `Longrein-Post-Onboarding-Checkins.md` Month 3 template, you've already asked: *"Per your founding-member contract, you committed to introducing 2 stables in [country]. Who's the first one?"*

If they replied with a name → go to Step 2. If they went vague ("I'll think about it") → go to Step 1.5.

### Step 1.5 — Lower the bar (only if needed)

Send 7 days after the Month 3 email:

> Hi [first name],
>
> Following up on the referral. Realised I was asking the wrong question.
>
> Easier version: among the stable owners you talk to in a normal month, who's the **most organised**? Doesn't matter if you think they "need" Longrein — just the one who actually runs their place professionally.
>
> Tell me their first name + stable. I'll figure out if they're a fit and only reach out if yes. You're not committing them to anything by naming them. — Andreja

**Why this works:** "Who's the most organised" is easier to answer than "who would buy Longrein". You filter for fit yourself.

## Step 2 — The intro template the founding member sends

Once they name a stable, you send THIS to the founding member, with all bracketed fields pre-filled. They forward it (they don't write their own — that's where momentum dies).

**Subject the founding member sends:** Have you seen Longrein?

> Hi [target stable owner first name],
>
> Quick one — I've been using a thing called Longrein for the past [N] months at [my stable name]. Built specifically for stables like ours in [country]. It handles lessons, payments, boarding charges, and has a welfare module that flags when a horse is over-ridden.
>
> I'm not affiliated, no commission. I just trust the founder (Andreja, runs her own stable in Vilnius) and Longrein has saved me [X hours/week] on admin. Thought you'd want to know.
>
> She's still in founding-member phase — handful of stables on for free. Worth a 20-min call if you're curious. Her email: hello@longrein.eu.
>
> [signed by founding member]

**The pre-fill workflow:**

1. Open the founding member's tracker row. Pull: months they've been on Longrein, hours/week saved (from their Q1 1-pager), their stable name.
2. Compose the intro with their data. Preview in your draft.
3. Send to the founding member with this cover note:

> Hi [FM first name],
>
> Pre-wrote the intro for [target stable]. Forward as-is or edit in 30 seconds — your call. Putting it in your hands so you don't have to compose under decision fatigue.
>
> When they reply to you, just CC me and I'll take it from there. — Andreja

**Why this works:** the founding member doesn't have to write or think. The 30-second cost is just clicking forward.

## Step 3 — When the target stable replies (to founding member, CC'd to you)

Within 4 hours of the CC, you reply directly:

> Hi [target first name],
>
> Andreja from Longrein here — [FM name] introduced us. Thank you for being open to a chat.
>
> Two ways to do this:
>
> 1. **20-min Zoom** to see Longrein on screen — pick a slot here: [Cal.com URL]
> 2. **Quick written Q&A** — reply with your top 3 questions and I'll answer with screenshots within 24h
>
> Whichever feels lower-friction. — Andreja

**Why two options:** some stable owners hate Zoom calls but will engage in writing. By offering both, you double conversion.

**Don't pitch in the first email.** The CC'd intro already did 80% of the work; jumping into features here is what kills warm leads.

## Step 4 — The first call with a referred stable

Use `Longrein-Onboarding-Zoom-Script.md` — but with one modification at the start:

**Open the call with:**

> Before I show you anything, I want to know: what did [referring FM] say about Longrein that made you say yes to this call? I'm asking because their reason might or might not match yours, and I want to talk to your reason, not theirs.

**This single question:**
- Tells you what's resonating in word-of-mouth (positioning gold)
- Filters out "I'm just being polite to [FM]" stables (don't waste 30 min on those)
- Signals you take their time seriously

If the answer is hollow ("they said it was good"): "What's the part of running your stable that's currently most painful? Let's see if Longrein actually addresses that — and if not, I'll tell you straight."

If the answer is specific ("they said the welfare module saves them from over-riding horses"): you have a perfect demo focal point.

## Step 5 — The follow-up either way (after the call)

### If they want to onboard

Send the founding-member terms IF they're in the first 10 (slots 1–10). Otherwise, send the **post-FM founding-second-cohort** terms:

| Cohort | Free period | Locked price | Conditions |
|---|---|---|---|
| FM 1–10 | 12 months | €30/mo lifetime | 2 referrals, 1 case study |
| FM 11–25 (referrals only) | 6 months | €40/mo for 3 years | 1 case study at month 6 |
| Public (post-FM) | 14-day trial | full €60/mo | none |

**Slot 11+ stables are not founding members.** They get a more limited deal — but still better than public, because they came warm. Don't conflate.

### If they don't want to onboard right now

> Hi [first name],
>
> Thank you for the time. No pressure on timing. I'll send you one short email per quarter on what's new in Longrein — feel free to ignore until something useful catches you.
>
> Whatever you decide, don't switch to anything before talking to [referring FM] again about how their experience evolved over the year. They're the most honest data point you have.
>
> — Andreja

**Then add to a "warm-but-cold" list. One quarterly email — never more.** This is the long-tail conversion engine.

## Step 6 — Loop back to the referring founding member

Within 24h of the target stable's first call (yes or no), email the FM:

**If target said yes:**

> Hi [FM first name],
>
> Quick update: [target stable] just signed on. They're starting [date]. That's referral #1 from your contract — one to go.
>
> Your name will be on `longrein.eu` as their referrer once they hit the 30-day mark, with your permission. Thank you, genuinely.
>
> — Andreja

**If target said no:**

> Hi [FM first name],
>
> [Target stable] decided not to come on right now. The reason was [specific — share what's not confidential]. I'm grateful for the intro regardless — it tells me something about positioning.
>
> Don't worry about it counting against your 2-referral commitment. The commitment is "introduce 2 stables", not "convert 2 stables". You did your part.
>
> One more in your back pocket? — Andreja

**Why loop back even when no:** the FM feels seen. Without this loop, they assume the worst (their referral was bad, you didn't try hard enough, etc.). With this loop, they're more likely to send referral #2.

## A separate path — strategic referrals (federations, trade media, vets)

Beyond the 10 founding members' personal networks, there are 4 "node" relationships in each country that unlock 5–20 stables each:

| Node | Why they refer | What you offer |
|---|---|---|
| National equestrian federation | Members ask them for software recommendations | Free Longrein for federation use + branded co-marketing post on `longrein.eu` |
| Equestrian trade publication / blog | Content-hungry, wants exclusive product features | First-look interviews, exclusive feature reveals |
| Equine vet practice (regional) | Welfare module aligns with their concerns | Co-author a 1-pager on horse over-use, distributed to all their client stables |
| Equestrian saddle / equipment retailer | Sees who's running stables professionally | Cross-promotion at customer events |

For each, send the LinkedIn or email outreach **after** you have at least 3 founding members in their country. Not before — without social proof, these conversations stall.

Template for federation:

**Subject:** Welfare data from Lithuanian stables — interested?

> Hi [federation contact name],
>
> Andreja from Longrein. We've been running 3 founding-member stables in Lithuania for 4 months now. Quick observation that might interest the federation:
>
> Across these stables, our welfare module flagged [N] over-cap incidents and [N] resting-too-long incidents. None of these were caught by paper records before Longrein. Welfare is a shared concern between us and you.
>
> Two questions:
>
> 1. Would the federation find a (free, anonymized) quarterly welfare report useful for member education?
> 2. If we co-developed national benchmarks, would the federation be willing to recommend Longrein to member stables?
>
> Open to a 30-min call in [city] this month. — Andreja

**Why this works:** leads with data they don't have, asks instead of pitches, offers legitimacy in exchange for distribution. The model is "welfare partnership", not "software vendor".

## What NOT to do

- **Don't ask FMs for >2 referrals in the first year.** That's the contract. Asking for more makes them feel used.
- **Don't pay referral commissions during founding-member phase.** It cheapens the relationship and signals you're commission-driven, not relationship-driven. Commissions become appropriate at month 12+ if at all.
- **Don't reach out to a referred stable behind the FM's back.** Always CC, always "introduced by [FM]". The FM's social capital is what makes this work.
- **Don't accept a referral and then ghost.** Within 24h reply, every time, no exceptions.
- **Don't push when a target says no.** One follow-up at quarter end, then drop. Pushing converts 1% and burns 99% of word-of-mouth credibility.

## After month 6 — measure honestly

Per FM, count:
- Number of intros sent
- Number that converted to a demo
- Number that converted to paid

Across all 10 FMs:
- Total intros: target ≥15 (out of theoretical 20)
- Total demos: target ≥8
- Total paying stables: target ≥4 → that's 40% conversion from warm intro, the industry benchmark

If you're below: the intro template is wrong, OR your founding members aren't getting enough value to refer with conviction. Both are fixable; both require honest review.
