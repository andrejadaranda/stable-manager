# Post-onboarding check-ins — retention playbook

**Pre-written follow-up emails for founding members at Day 7, 14, 30, Month 3, 6, 12. Send these manually — don't automate. The whole point is that the founding member feels like a real human is paying attention.**

> Voice rule on every send: warm, direct, under 150 words. One question per email. Never include a feature list. Never sell.

---

## Why this exists

The audit flagged post-launch retention as the biggest risk of the founding-member program. You'll spend 60–80 hours onboarding 10 stables in May–June. If half of them go quiet by month 3, you've burned the calendar with no case studies, no referrals, no testimonials.

These check-ins are the difference between a founding member who renews at month 13 and one who silently churns at month 6.

**Cadence rule:** if a founding member replies to ANY check-in, drop them out of the auto-cadence and treat them as an active conversation. Resume the cadence only if they go quiet for 4+ weeks.

---

## Day 7 — "How was the first week?"

**When to send:** Exactly 7 days after their onboarding Zoom. Tuesday or Wednesday morning, never Monday or Friday.

**Subject:** Quick check-in — how was week 1?

**Body:**

> Hi [first name],
>
> A week in. Two questions:
>
> 1. What's the one thing in Longrein that's already saving you time?
> 2. What's the one thing that's annoying you?
>
> No need for a long answer. Three sentences each is enough. The annoying thing is the more useful one — that's what I fix this week.
>
> — Andreja

**Why this works:** Two specific questions force a concrete answer. Asking for the annoying thing gives them permission to be honest. The promise to fix it that week is what differentiates founder-led product from polished-but-impersonal SaaS.

**What to do with replies:**
- "Saving time on X" → save the quote in the tracker. This is testimonial fuel.
- "Annoyed by Y" → fix it that week. Reply with "Fixed. Try again." Nothing else. The action is the message.

## Day 14 — "Are your clients using the portal?"

**When to send:** 14 days after onboarding. The portal adoption question is the leading indicator of whether the stable owner is championing Longrein internally.

**Subject:** Are your riders logging in yet?

**Body:**

> Hi [first name],
>
> Two weeks in. Most founding members hit the same plateau around now: you're using the calendar daily, but your clients aren't logging into the portal yet.
>
> Quick check: roughly how many of your clients have signed in this week?
>
> If it's under half, I have a one-line WhatsApp template that gets adoption from ~30% to ~80% in a week. Want it?
>
> — Andreja

**Why this works:** Names the exact pattern they're in (so they feel seen, not generic), gives a concrete benchmark (~50%), offers a tiny no-pressure value-add (one-line template), ends with one yes/no question.

**What to send if they say yes:**

> Send each of your clients on WhatsApp tonight:
>
> "Hi [name], from now on you can see your lessons, payments, and your kid's progress here: [your stable URL]/login. Login uses the same email I have for you. Reply if it doesn't work."
>
> Personal, useful, and they'll log in within 24 hours. Most stables hit 80% adoption from this single message.

## Day 30 — "30-min review call"

**When to send:** 30 days after onboarding. This is THE call. The audit is right that a 20-min call at month 2 saves a churn at month 4 — month 1 is even better.

**Subject:** 30-day review — 20 min on Zoom?

**Body:**

> Hi [first name],
>
> One month in. Time for the 20-min review I promised on day one.
>
> What I want to cover:
> - What's working for you in Longrein
> - What's frustrating
> - What you'd build next if you were me
>
> Pick a slot here: [Cal.com /review URL]
>
> If 20 min is too much, reply with the three answers in writing. Either works.
>
> — Andreja

**Why this works:** The 30-day call is a contractual obligation in the founding-member offer (1 of the 6 onboarding touchpoints). Naming it "review" not "check-in" signals it's structured, not a courtesy chat. Offering a written-only fallback respects their time.

**On the call — three questions, in order:**
1. "What does Longrein do for you that nothing else did?" (positioning gold)
2. "What's the moment in your week where Longrein still fails you?" (roadmap input)
3. "If you had to lose Longrein tomorrow, what would you miss most?" (the "ouch test" — Sean Ellis style)

If question 3 has a weak answer, you have a retention problem you haven't seen yet. Schedule a deeper call within 2 weeks.

**After the call:** within 24 hours, send a 4-bullet recap in writing. They forward this internally to their team. That's the artefact that makes them champion you.

## Month 3 — "Quarterly check-in + first ask"

**When to send:** 3 months after onboarding. By now they have data: 12 weeks of lessons, 3 boarding cycles, real welfare flags. This is where you ask for the first hard thing — a referral.

**Subject:** Quarter 1 done — quick ask

**Body:**

> Hi [first name],
>
> Three months in. Two things.
>
> First: I owe you a 1-pager on what Longrein has done for your stable so far — total lessons, revenue logged, hours saved on admin. I'll send it Friday. Read it, share with your accountant if useful.
>
> Second: per your founding-member contract, you committed to introducing 2 stables in [country]. Who's the first one? I just need a first name + their stable name. I'll take it from there.
>
> Reply with one name today, even if you're 70% sure.
>
> — Andreja

**Why this works:** Leads with what you're delivering to them (the 1-pager — non-negotiable, must actually send by Friday), THEN asks for the referral. Reminding them this was in their contract removes the awkwardness. "70% sure" makes the bar trivially low.

**The 1-pager you must send Friday:** Use Settings → Reports in Longrein to pull their actual numbers. Format:

```
Longrein impact — [stable name] — Q1 2026
- Lessons logged: [number]
- Revenue tracked: €[amount]
- Welfare flags caught: [number] over-cap blocks, [number] resting-too-long alerts
- Hours saved on admin (estimate): [hours] per week
- What changed for you: [one sentence from their day-30 call]
```

This 1-pager is the single most powerful retention artefact. It makes month 13 (when free-period ends) feel like a continuation, not a decision.

## Month 6 — "Six months — let's talk"

**When to send:** Exactly 6 months after onboarding. This is the contract midpoint. From here, every conversation is about month 13 and beyond.

**Subject:** Six months — a real conversation

**Body:**

> Hi [first name],
>
> Six months in. We're past the polite phase.
>
> I want a 45-min call. Three things on the agenda:
>
> 1. The 6-month version of your 1-pager (sent below).
> 2. What you'd need from Longrein in months 7–12 to keep saying yes at month 13.
> 3. The 2 case-study questions I want to put on longrein.eu — with your permission and your name on it.
>
> Pick a slot: [Cal.com /sixmonth URL]
>
> Skip if you must, but I'd rather you push back to month 7 than skip entirely.
>
> — Andreja

**Why this works:** Direct framing ("past the polite phase") earns honesty back. Pre-loading agenda removes friction. Asking permission for the case study is the moment you turn user → public reference.

**What you're listening for on the call:**
- A genuine "yes, I'll renew at month 13 at €30/mo" — book it now, mark in tracker
- A hesitant "yes, probably" — they need 1–2 more wins before month 13. What are those wins?
- An honest "I'm not sure" — this is the GIFT. Ask exactly what would tip them. Build that thing.

**Don't ask "will you renew?" directly.** Ask "what would have to be true at month 13 for this to be an obvious yes?" The answer is your roadmap.

## Month 9 — "Three months out from renewal"

**When to send:** Month 9. Short. Logistical, not emotional.

**Subject:** Three months from renewal — anything to flag?

**Body:**

> Hi [first name],
>
> Three months from your founding-member period ending (month 13: [exact date]). At that point you move to €30/mo for the lifetime of your account, locked in.
>
> If anything has changed for your stable — closing, scaling, switching tools, budget freeze — tell me now, not at month 12. I'll plan accordingly and won't surprise you.
>
> If everything's on track, no need to reply.
>
> — Andreja

**Why this works:** Lowers stakes ("no need to reply"). Confirms the price ahead of time so there's no shock. Treats them like an adult who's allowed to leave. The honesty here is what builds the case for staying.

## Month 12 — "The renewal conversation"

**When to send:** Exactly 30 days before their free period ends. This is the conversion moment.

**Subject:** Month 13 starts on [date] — confirming €30/mo

**Body:**

> Hi [first name],
>
> Your founding-member free period ends on [exact date]. From [date+1], your account moves to €30/mo (50% of public price, locked for the lifetime of the account, per your founding-member contract).
>
> What I need from you:
>
> 1. Card on file via [Stripe link] — takes 2 min, charges nothing today.
> 2. A reply to this email saying "yes, going to month 13."
>
> If you're not continuing, also tell me — by email or 10-min call. I want to know the real reason and I won't fight it.
>
> Either way, your data stays exportable for 90 days after.
>
> — Andreja

**Why this works:** No salesy language. The price is already known (you confirmed at month 9). The two clear actions remove decision friction. Inviting honest exit-feedback signals you're confident enough to handle no.

**If they say yes:** great. Move them out of the founding-member tracker into the regular customer cadence. Drop a thank-you handwritten card in the post within 7 days (not email — physical). They'll send a photo of it on Instagram and that's worth 10 paid-acquisition LinkedIn posts.

**If they say no:** thank them. Ask the 4 churn-research questions on a 20-min call:
1. When did you first feel this might not work?
2. What were you hoping Longrein would do that it didn't?
3. What are you switching to (or back to)?
4. Would you have stayed if [specific thing] were different?

Document every word. This is your most valuable feedback all year.

## Special-case sends (use as needed, not on cadence)

### When you ship something they asked for

> Hi [first name],
>
> Three weeks ago you said [the thing]. It shipped this morning.
>
> Try it: [link / Settings path].
>
> Tell me if I got it wrong. — Andreja

**Why:** This is the single highest-trust signal a founder can send. Use it every time.

### When they go quiet (no login for 14+ days)

> Hi [first name],
>
> I noticed you haven't logged into Longrein in two weeks. Three possibilities:
>
> 1. You're on holiday — enjoy.
> 2. You delegated to your trainer — perfect, that's what the Employee role is for.
> 3. Something is broken or frustrating — tell me what.
>
> Reply with 1, 2, or 3.
>
> — Andreja

**Why:** Names all three options non-defensively. The single-digit reply is friction-free. Anyone choosing 3 needs an immediate phone call.

### When they hit a milestone (100th lesson, first quarter profitable, etc.)

> Hi [first name],
>
> Just saw — you logged your 100th lesson on Longrein this morning (since [start date], averaging [X]/week). Worth a screenshot for your team.
>
> Genuine thanks for trusting this thing in its first year. — Andreja

**Why:** Founder-noticed milestones are unforgettable. Pull the data manually from the dashboard. Don't automate — the moment you automate this, it's worthless.

## What NOT to send

- "Hi [name], we hope you're doing well…" (corporate, no signal)
- Newsletters with feature dumps (founding members don't need a changelog email — they're talking to you)
- "Have you tried [feature X]?" without any context about why it's relevant to them specifically
- Anything that sounds like a marketing automation tool wrote it

If you're not 100% sure the email is human-from-Andreja-feeling, don't send it.

## The retention math

10 founding members → at month 13:
- 7 renew at €30/mo = €2,520/year
- 2 churn (with feedback that fixes the next 100)
- 1 upgrades or refers another stable

That's not where Longrein makes money. The 7 stay because they're public references that make outreach #11–#100 trivial. Each retained founding member is worth €5K–€10K in saved acquisition cost over the next 18 months.

This document is how you protect that.
