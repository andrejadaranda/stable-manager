The five-tool problem

Right now your stable is run on five tools that do not talk to each other.

Excel for the lesson schedule. WhatsApp for last-minute changes. A paper diary in the office. A Messenger group for the trainers. A printed invoice book at the end of the month. Every tool works alone. Together, they leak hours, money, and — sometimes — a tired horse who goes out one ride too many.

Hoofbeat replaces all five with one screen.
Three things on one screen

The only stable tool that puts horse welfare on the same screen as the schedule and the invoice.

01 — Workload

See which horse worked too much this week. Before anyone else does.

Every horse gets a workload heatmap based on lessons, hacks, training, and turnout. The dashboard turns red before a horse gets sour or sore. Welfare and operations on the same screen — for the first time.

02 — Schedule

Lessons, livery, vet, farrier — one calendar, no double-bookings.

Drag-and-drop scheduling. Recurring lessons in one tap. Clients see their bookings on their phone. The database physically prevents double-bookings — not as a check, as a guarantee. Email reminders shipping in beta; SMS shipping at public launch.

03 — Payments

One ledger for lessons, packs, and boarding — and a printable invoice in two clicks.

Recurring boarding charges, lesson packs with remaining-balance tracking, single invoices. Print-ready A4 invoice and weekly schedule for the barn wall. Client balance is always live. SEPA and Stripe Checkout shipping at public launch — early-access stables get priority access.

Five questions

The things stable owners ask before they sign anything.

When does Hoofbeat launch?

Public beta opens in autumn 2026. Waitlist members get access first, and lock in the launch price for 12 months.

Is my data safe?

Yes. EU-hosted (Frankfurt). GDPR-compliant from day one. One click to export everything as a CSV. One click to delete everything if you ever leave. Your data is yours.

Will it work on my phone?

Yes — that is where most of it is designed for. Trainers check in lessons in the arena from the phone. Clients see schedules and pay invoices on theirs. The desktop is the back office; the phone is the workplace.

What if I run a small backyard yard with three horses?

Hoofbeat is built for stables with 15–40 horses. If yours is smaller, the Starter plan still works — but the product is most useful once you have at least one trainer who is not you.

Can I see it before I commit?

Yes. Get on the waitlist and you'll receive a 90-second tour and a 1-on-1 onboarding call with the founder for the first 50 customers. After that, it's a 14-day free trial — no card.
