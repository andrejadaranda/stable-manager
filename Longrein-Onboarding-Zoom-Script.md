# Founding Members onboarding — 45-min Zoom script

**For Andreja. Run once per stable. Total: 45 min target. Print this; keep open in second screen.**

> Voice rule: direct, calm, slow. You are not selling — they already said yes. You're handing over a tool you built. Treat them as colleagues, not customers.

---

## 0:00–0:03 · Open warmly, set frame

> "Thanks for making time. I'll keep us to 45 minutes — first 15 we look at your stable as it stands now, next 20 we set up Longrein so it actually fits, last 10 you ask me anything. Sound okay?"

Open camera. Mute notifications. Have a horse photo behind you if you can — they'll like that.

---

## 0:03–0:18 · Their reality (15 min listening, almost no talking)

Three open questions. Let them ramble. Take notes — not for them, for the Sav. 1 product cycle.

1. **"Walk me through a normal Tuesday at the stable. Start at 6am."**
   Listen for: WhatsApp groups, paper diaries, double-bookings, who pays for what, who chases unpaid lessons.

2. **"What's the one thing about running this place that makes you tired in a way that doesn't feel earned?"**
   This surfaces the friction they actually pay to remove. Often it's not what they expected.

3. **"What about your horses worries you most when you can't see the schedule?"**
   This is your welfare wedge — let them tell you, don't pitch it.

> Don't promise Longrein solves any specific thing in this segment. Just listen.

**End of segment:** "Okay, thank you. That helps me a lot. Let me show you what we built."

---

## 0:18–0:38 · Set up Longrein with their data (20 min)

Share screen. Open `app.longrein.eu/login` — sign them in to the account you created for them in advance using the email they confirmed.

### Five things to show, in this exact order

**1. Welfare dashboard (4 min) — the wedge**
- Open `/dashboard/welfare`
- Show the 5-bucket strip: Over cap / Near cap / Resting / Steady / Light
- "This is the only screen no other stable software puts at the top of the navigation. It's the reason we built Longrein — because the question 'is this horse working too much this week?' should never need a spreadsheet."
- Click one tile. Show the load %, days since last ride, the auto-reminder.
- Their reaction is your single best signal. If they lean in, you have a customer for life. If they shrug, push more on the workload story.

**2. Calendar (3 min)**
- Open `/dashboard/calendar`
- Drag a lesson to a different time. "Database-level guarantee — you can't double-book a horse or trainer. Not as a check, as a contract."
- Show day view, week view, print view (one click — print the schedule for the wall).

**3. Horse profile (4 min)**
- Open one horse. Show Overview / Sessions / Health / Boarding tabs.
- "Every lesson, every session, every vet visit — one screen. No more searching three places."
- Show the per-horse profitability link if relevant (`/dashboard/horses/profitability`).

**4. Lesson packages + Client portal (4 min)**
- Open one client.
- "She has a 10-pack. 7 lessons remaining. Auto-deducted when you mark a lesson complete."
- Show the client portal view from her perspective — `/my-lessons`, `/my-payments`.
- "Your clients will check this themselves. You stop fielding 'how many lessons do I have left' WhatsApp messages."

**5. Payments + boarding (3 min)**
- Open `/dashboard/payments` and `/dashboard/settings/boarding`.
- "Outstanding boarders board — click any unpaid → pick payment method → done."
- Generate boarding charges for the month (one click).

**6. Settings → Features (1 min)**
- "If you don't use Chat, turn it off. If you don't do boarding, turn it off. The sidebar adapts."
- Highlight: Chat is OFF by default — point it out only if they ask.

**7. Skip these unless they ask:**
- Cmd+K search (premium signal but not needed in onboarding)
- 2FA (founding members can enable later)
- CSV import (offer to handle this offline)

### Set them up to actually use it tomorrow

Pick **one** of these three migration paths based on their answer to "How are you tracking lessons today?":

**Path A: They use a paper diary or Excel.**
- "Send me your Excel today. I'll convert it to a clean CSV import overnight and email back the import-ready file. You upload tomorrow morning, your stable is live."

**Path B: They use Equine Genie / Stablebuzz / similar.**
- "Export horses + clients from there. Send me the file. Same overnight conversion. Same upload tomorrow."

**Path C: They run on WhatsApp + memory.**
- "Easiest of the three. Tomorrow morning I'll send a 5-step guide: add 5 horses, add 5 clients, schedule 5 lessons. By lunch you have a baseline. Walk before you run."

## 0:38–0:43 · Their questions (5 min)

> Don't anticipate questions. Wait for them.

Most common questions and short, direct answers:

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| "What if you go bust?" | "All your data is exportable as CSV any time from Settings → Backup. You own it. Worst case you import it elsewhere." |
| "Can my trainer use it on her phone?" | "Yes — the calendar and session logging are phone-first. We're running our own dogfood week on phone-only this Sav." |
| "Do you support [country] tax / VAT?" | "Right now invoices are PDF print, not VAT-compliant. We add that Q4'26. If your accountant needs a specific format, send me a sample after the call — we'll cover it before you need it." |
| "What happens at month 13?" | "You stay on the founding-member rate — capped at 50% of public price for life. Currently that means €30/mo for stables with up to ~25 horses. Locked in writing." |
| "Can I add my employees?" | "Yes. Settings → Team → Invite. They sign in with their own email. Owner sees everything, employee sees their assigned horses + lessons." |
| "What if I want to leave?" | "Settings → Account → Close. CSV export stays available 30 days after closing." |

## 0:43–0:45 · Close + next 7 days

> "Three things from me, three things from you."

**From me:**
1. Within 24 hours: an email summarising what we agreed today + the migration path we picked
2. Within 48 hours: your account is fully populated with horses + clients + price list
3. Within 7 days: a short check-in WhatsApp from me — "How's it going?" — answer when convenient

**From you:**
1. **One real lesson logged today.** Even just one. Don't wait for full migration.
2. **One client invited to the portal** so they see it from the rider side
3. **One thing that bugged you in this 45 minutes** sent to me by email — anything from "the welfare colours are too pale" to "I can't find X"

**Last line:** "Thanks for trusting us with this. You're founding member 1 of 10 — every change in the product over the next 12 months gets your fingerprints on it."

End call.

---

## What NOT to do during the call

- Don't talk about pricing. They've already agreed to 12 months free.
- Don't promise features. Even "we're working on X" — say "noted, no promise on timing" instead.
- Don't apologise for what's missing. Say "yes, that's not in yet — I'll add it to the list."
- Don't compare to competitors. They picked you; they don't need to be sold against Equine Genie.
- Don't show the Chat module. It's flagged off and we deprioritised it.
- Don't show fake-decoration anywhere. If a screen has placeholder data, skip it.

---

## Tone reminders

- Slow your speech. Founding members are tired stable owners; they speak slowly. Match them.
- Use their words. If they say "lesson packs" not "packages," switch to their vocabulary.
- If something breaks during the demo, name it: "There's the friction. I'll fix this by Friday." Don't pretend.
- End every section with a small breath, not a sales close. They'll volunteer their next question.
