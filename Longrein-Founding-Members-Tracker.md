**Status enum:**
- `Cold` — outreach not yet sent
- `Sent` — outreach email sent, waiting for reply
- `Replied` — they replied, demo not yet booked
- `Scheduled` — demo on the calendar
- `Onboarded` — demo done, account live, first lesson logged
- `Active` — using the product, attending monthly calls
- `Churned` — stopped using; reason in Notes
- `Cold-2` — no reply after 14 days, in autumn-2 round

## Weekly rhythm (every Monday morning, 30 min)

1. **Open this file.** Sort by Status — focus first on `Replied` and `Scheduled` rows.
2. **Send Day-4 bumps** to anyone in `Sent` status for 4+ days.
3. **Send Day-14 finals** to anyone in `Sent` for 14+ days.
4. **Confirm next-week demos** by WhatsApp the day before.
5. **Update Status enum** for everyone who moved.
6. **Pull one quote** from the previous week's calls and paste it into the brand quote bank (`/APP/brand/quotes.md` if it exists, or just keep here).

---

## Risk triggers (act immediately if any one fires)

- **Day 7 after demo, no first lesson logged** → WhatsApp them with one specific question about their migration: "Did the CSV import work or do you want me to do it for you over Zoom Friday morning?"
- **Day 30 of account, fewer than 5 lessons logged** → 1:1 phone call: "What's stopping you from using it? Be honest." Document their answer in Notes.
- **Two missed monthly check-ins in a row** → They're churning. One call to ask why. If they say "we just didn't get around to migrating," accept it gracefully and offer to either (a) extend the trial 3 months past the migration date, or (b) close cleanly with a clean export.
- **They say "we're going back to Excel"** → That's a product feedback signal more valuable than money. 60-min call, no agenda, you listen. Take a 2-page postmortem.

## What this tracker is NOT

- It is not a CRM. Don't add deal stages, won/lost, ARR. These are colleagues, not prospects.
- It is not for VC reporting. The founding-member period is product-led, not metrics-led.
- It is not for hiring decisions. Don't conclude "founding member 4 was high-effort therefore we shouldn't take low-touch customers." Founding members are deliberately high-effort because their feedback is high-value.

This is a memory aid for one founder onboarding 10 stables manually over 12 months. Keep it simple.
