## SEKCIJA 2 — Path B (real company) deep dive

Šis sekcija detalizuoja Path B (DACH launch + first hire), nes tai labiausiai tikėtinas kelias jei Year-1 actuals OK.

### 2.1 Year-2 Financial Model (Path B, LIKELY case)

**Predictable revenue (LT base):**
- M12 baseline: 35 customers @ €87/mo avg = €3,045 MRR
- Net new LT M13–M24: +25 customers (slower growth nei Year 1, market saturation)
- Churn: 10% annual (3–4 customers leave Year 2)
- M24 LT: 56 customers @ €92/mo avg = €5,150 MRR (LT ARR ~€62K)

**New revenue (DACH, M17+):**
- M17 (soft launch): 5 founding DE customers FREE (12 mo pilot)
- M18 (public launch): 3 paid DE customers @ €120/mo = €360 MRR
- M21: 12 DE customers @ €110/mo = €1,320 MRR
- M24: 25 DE customers @ €115/mo = €2,875 MRR (DACH ARR ~€34.5K)

**Combined M24:**
- Total customers: 56 LT + 25 DACH = 81 customers
- Total MRR: €5,150 + €2,875 = **€8,025 MRR**
- Total ARR: **~€96K**

**Costs (Year 2, Path B):**

| Category | Mėn vid | Year 2 total |
|---|---|---|
| Hosting (Supabase, Vercel, Resend) | €150 | €1,800 |
| Tools (Stripe, Crisp, Plausible, Notion) | €200 | €2,400 |
| Hire #1 (DE-native CS/product, FT) | €4,500 | €54,000 |
| Hire #1 social/payroll taxes (LT contracted) | €1,500 | €18,000 |
| Marketing/ads (LT + DACH) | €1,500 | €18,000 |
| Legal/accounting | €400 | €4,800 |
| Travel (DACH events, customer visits) | €500 | €6,000 |
| Andreja salary (founder draw) | €2,000 | €24,000 |
| **Total burn Year 2** | **€10,750** | **€129,000** |

**Net Year 2 (Path B, LIKELY):**
- Revenue: ~€60K (annualized from MRR ramp)
- Burn: ~€129K
- **Net loss: ~€69K** ← reikia kapitalo arba angel investment

**Implication:** Path B reikalauja **€80–100K** Year-2 funding (cash buffer + €70K loss). Tai yra fundraise decision.

### 2.2 Hire #1 — kas, kada, kiek?

**Profile (priority order):**

**Option A: DE-native Customer Success / Product Specialist** (preferred)
- LT/DE/EN — kalba bilingual
- Equestrian background (savininkė arba ridau 5+ metų)
- 3–5 metų SaaS arba customer success patirtis
- Self-starter, remote-first
- Comp: €4,000–4,500/mo gross (LT contractor B2B), or €3,500/mo + 0.5–1.0% equity

**Where to find:**
- Lithuanian dressur/jumping community su DE connections
- LinkedIn DE search: "customer success" + "SaaS" + "equestrian" / "horse" / "Reitsport"
- Equestrian forums (Reiterforum.de, Pferd.de) — soft outreach
- Word-of-mouth Lithuanian-Vokiečių diaspora

**Recruiting timeline:**
- M14 start sourcing (post job, network outreach)
- M15 first interviews (5–10 candidates)
- M16 final interviews (2–3 finalists, 2-week trial work)
- M17 offer + start
- M18 fully onboarded, owns DACH customer relationships

**Risk if you delay hire to M18+:**
- DACH launch quality suffers (you can't do EN, DE, sales, product, support solo)
- Burnout escalates (already 65h/wk Year 1, +DACH = 80h/wk = unsustainable)
- DE customers churn faster (no native-speaker support = pain)

**Risk if you hire too early (M13–M14):**
- No real workload yet for new hire (DACH not launched)
- Burn rate escalates before revenue
- Mishire risk (you don't know yet what role DACH actually needs)

**Sweet spot:** **M15–M16 hire**, fully ramped by M18 launch.

### 2.3 Fundraise vs Bootstrap decision (M16)

**Three options M16:**

#### Option 1: Pure bootstrap (no external capital)
- Personal funds + Year-2 revenue covers
- Hire #1 deferred to M22 (after revenue ramps)
- DACH launch slower (M21 instead of M18)
- Year-2 ARR target: €70K (not €96K)
- **Pros:** 100% ownership, no investor pressure
- **Cons:** slower, more burnout risk, miss DACH window

#### Option 2: Angel round €150–300K (preferred)
- 3–5 angel investors, €30–60K each
- Convertible note (no valuation set, converts at Series A)
- Or SAFE (Y Combinator standard)
- 5–10% dilution effective
- Use of funds: hire #1 (€60K), DACH marketing (€20K), runway buffer (€70K)
- **Pros:** speed, DACH on schedule, angel network value
- **Cons:** equity dilution, reporting overhead, exit pressure

#### Option 3: Series A €1.5M (premature)
- VC-led round, €1.5M at €5–8M post-money valuation
- 20–25% dilution
- Hire 3–4 people Year 2
- DACH expansion + product line expansion
- **Pros:** real war chest, professionalization
- **Cons:** likely premature for €60K ARR Year-2 start, VC pressure to scale fast may break sustainable rhythm

**My recommendation (M5 perspective, will revisit M11):**
- Path B + Option 2 (angel round €200–250K)
- Bootstrap mentality with capital cushion
- DACH-focused use of funds

**Anti-recommendation:** Series A Year 2. Šis kategoriškai per anksti — tipinis Series A signal'as €1M+ ARR, ne €60K. VC'iai į mažus deals neeis, o jei eis — tu prarandi optionality (jie tave varys į scale, kuris sulaužys product-market fit).

### 2.4 DACH go-to-market sub-strategy

**Why Germany first (not Austria/Switzerland):**
- DE: 1.2M sport horses, 7,000+ commercial stables, premium SaaS spending culture
- AT/CH: smaller markets, but easier for follow-on (same language, similar regulations)

**Channel strategy:**

**Positioning (DACH-specific):**

- DACH positioning: "GDPR-native, EU-only data, premium support" (vs. US competitors)
- Premium price ladder: €3.50/horse DE vs €3.00 LT (DACH market accepts higher)

## SEKCIJA 4 — Path C (build-to-flip) — strateginis acquisition target

Path C reiškia **statyti** Longrein nesireinaminančiai konsolidacijai. Tai validus, bet specifinis kelias.

**Kada Path C tampa realus:**
- Year-1 actuals 100%+ LIKELY (overperformance signal)
- Industrija konsoliduojasi: konkurentai (Equilab, EquineApp, Hippomon, kt.) susilieja arba parduodami
- Strategic acquirer'iai pradeda mėgstamą pirkimo logiką (Veterinary software roll-up, FarmOS-equivalent in equestrian)
- Tu sąmoningai ne nori 5–10 metų ilgo SaaS path'o, bet vidutinio (3–4 metai)

**Path C strategy (M13–M30):**
- Path B operations (DACH, hire, fundraise) bet **build with acquisition optics**
- Aukšta product quality, low tech debt (acquirer due diligence pass)
- Customer relationships taikomos, NPS aukšta
- Brand recognition Vokiečių rinkoje (acquirer'iui yra value)
- Founder savaime prižiūri, kad klientų vertė neapsiremtų į ją asmeniškai (transferable)

**Acquisition price expectations:**
- M30 ARR €500K (LT + DACH combined)
- 5–7× ARR multiple (industry standard SaaS)
- = €2.5–3.5M sale price
- Founder net (after taxes, dilution): €1.5–2.5M

**Pros:**
- Predictable timeline (3–4 metai vs 7–10)
- Real money out
- Founder freedom post-sale

**Cons:**
- Acquirer pasirinkimas per anksti (M13) tave varo į specific decisions kurie lėtina natūralų augimą
- Lithuanian SaaS acquired by foreign company → potencialus sentiment'inis loss'as
- Year 3–4 už acquisition price'o tu negausi tiek, kiek standalone

**Recommendation:** Don't decide Path C until M18. Build B path, but if M18+ acquirer'iai aktyvuojasi — listen, don't dismiss.

## SEKCIJA 10 — Anti-patterns (kas paprastai breaks Year 2)

❌ **Hire too early.** M13 hire'as be revenue ramp = burn rate doom. Wait M15+ minimum.

❌ **Hire too late.** M20+ hire'as = burnout already happened, hire dirba fixing fires, ne building.

❌ **DACH "spray and pray" marketing.** €5K/mo DE Google Ads be product DE-native = waste. SEO + community first 6 mo.

❌ **Series A premature.** €60K ARR + Series A = signaling failure. Wait €1M+ ARR.

❌ **Product line expansion before core mature.** Marketplace at M16 when core has 50 bugs = death.

❌ **Founder pulls salary too aggressively.** €5K/mo founder draw in Year 2 = company starves. €2K is honest minimum.

❌ **Fundraise "just in case."** Raise when you have plan to deploy capital. Capital sitting in bank = clock ticking.

❌ **Skip Year-1 retro.** Going straight from M12 → M13 without 5-day off-grid retrospective = repeating Year-1 mistakes Year 2.

❌ **DACH price = LT price.** €3/horse DE pricing = leaving €30K/yr on table. DE pays €3.50–4.50.

❌ **Customer success = founder.** If hire #1 isn't customer-facing, bottleneck stays at you.
