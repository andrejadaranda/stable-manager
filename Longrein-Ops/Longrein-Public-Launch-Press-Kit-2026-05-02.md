# Longrein — Public Launch Press Kit

**Use:** Autumn 2026 public launch (target launch date: TBD — likely Sept-Nov 2026 depending on FM cohort 1 readiness).
**Goal:** Get 5+ press mentions in LT/PL/DE equestrian + tech press within 30 days of launch. Each mention = ~10–50 inbound signups + lasting SEO authority.
**Critical insight:** journalists work on 2-week lead time. Press materials must be ready 2 weeks BEFORE launch day, NOT on launch day.

---

## TL;DR

Press kit components (all in this doc):
1. **Press release template** — EN primary, LT/DE adaptations
2. **Journalist outreach list** — 30+ contacts ranked by priority
3. **Pitch email templates** — 4 versions per audience type
4. **Embargo + exclusive strategy** — how to give 1 publication first crack
5. **Media assets manifest** — what's needed (logo, photos, screenshots, founder bio)
6. **Quote bank** — pre-approved quotes for journalists to pull from
7. **Journalist FAQ** — answers to predictable questions
8. **Launch day social ammunition pack** — social posts + email sequences sync'd to press timing

Time investment: ~6-8 hours total (including journalist list research, pitch personalization, asset prep). Spread over 2 weeks pre-launch.

## Dalis 4 — Embargo + exclusive strategy

### Embargo concept

„Embargoed for [DATE], 09:00 LT time" — meaning: I'm sharing materials with you NOW, but you publish exactly [DATE]. Standard press practice.

**Why useful:** journalists love embargos because they can prepare deeper articles in advance.

**Implementation:**
1. Pitch email Tier 1 contacts: „I'm offering this story under embargo until [DATE]"
2. If they accept: send full press kit including draft press release + screenshots + quotes
3. They prepare article during 2-week embargo window
4. All publish on launch day simultaneously = bigger splash

### Exclusive concept

Even better than embargo: ONE publication gets the story FIRST, exclusive 24-48 hours before others.

**Pros:** that publication gets credit for breaking the story, dedicates more reporting effort, writes deeper article
**Cons:** other publications less motivated to cover (they're "second")

**My recommendation:** offer exclusive to ONE Tier 1 LT business press (e.g., Verslo žinios) IF they commit to:
- Long-form article (1500+ words)
- Founder interview included
- Dedicated section/feature placement

If they decline exclusive — fall back to embargo for all Tier 1.

### Decision matrix

```
IF Verslo žinios (or equivalent) commits to long-form exclusive:
  → Give them exclusive. Other Tier 1 get standard embargo for T+24h release.

ELSE IF 3+ Tier 1 publications interested:
  → Embargo all to launch day 09:00. Coordinated big splash.

ELSE IF <3 Tier 1 publications interested:
  → No embargo. Just send when ready. Hope for best.
```

**Q: What's the revenue?**
A: Founding Members are free for 12 months. First paid customer comes online with public launch. Pricing is €3/horse/month, with most yards in 15–40 horse range.

**Q: Who's your competition?**
A: US incumbents Equine Genie ($24/mo flat) and Stablebuzz ($30/mo flat), plus Excel + WhatsApp (free). We differentiate on European-built compliance (SEPA, GDPR, local language) and welfare-first product design.

**Q: What's your team size?**

**Q: How is Lithuanian about Lithuanian about Lithuania-built different?**
A: We start with the assumption that LT/PL/DACH stables have different operational realities than US stables — different VAT systems, different payment preferences (SEPA vs cards), different language needs, different welfare approaches. We build for those realities first.

**Q: Why do you talk so much about welfare?**
A: Because welfare is the lens through which the entire stable operates — but most software treats it as a reporting feature. We put workload tracking on the home screen because it should drive daily decisions, not weekly reviews.

**Q: When do you expand to DACH?**
A: Late 2027 / early 2028. We're focusing on Baltic + Polish saturation through Year 1, then preparing DACH-specific localization (German language, XRechnung, DE compliance) through 2027.

**Q: Are you concerned about competitors copying you?**

## Dalis 10 — Common launch press mistakes

1. **Sending press release blast to 100+ journalists** — they hate this. Personalize.
2. **Pitching journalists who don't cover your space** — research before pitching. Verslo žinios reporter who covers banking ≠ tech reporter.
3. **No embargo strategy** — risks chaotic publication timing. Coordinated launch hits harder.
4. **Press kit hosted in Google Drive (with permission gates)** — frustrates journalists. Public URL preferred.
5. **No quote bank** — journalists need quotes; if not provided, they paraphrase (often poorly).
6. **No follow-up** — journalists drop unanswered pitches. Polite follow-up after 1 week is normal practice.
7. **Excessive jargon** — „vertical SaaS for equine operations" loses journalists. „Software for stables" is clearer.
8. **No social sync** — press article links to dead social accounts = trust signal damage.
