# Longrein YouTube Video #1 — Full script + filming plan

**Title (locked):** „Why I'm building stable management software for Europe — from a riding club in Lithuania"
**Length target:** 8–10 minutes
**Audience:** Stable owners (primary), EU SaaS founders (secondary), equestrian industry observers (tertiary)
**Strategic role:** Evergreen authority builder. Once published, drives discovery for 12–24 months. Anchor for content engine. Acts as „credentials proof" for FM outreach + partner pitches + journalist conversations.
**Filming target date:** May 12–14 (Sav. 2 sprint, kai dogfood'iji Longrein TJK).
**Publishing target date:** May 23 (LAUNCH DAY — sinchronizuotai su FM beta launch'u).

## Strateginis kontekstas — kodėl YouTube būtinai

LinkedIn = networking + B2B credibility. Reach'as tik network'e.
IG = visual short-form. Reach'as priklauso nuo algorithmic luck.
YouTube = SEO-driven discovery. **Evergreen.** Uploadintas 2026, randamas 2028 paieška „European stable management software". Vienas video = 12–24 mėn. compound discovery surface.

Vertical SaaS founder'iai dažnai praleidžia YouTube nes „too much production." Šitas planas yra MINIMUM viable — telefono kameros, natural light, 1 location. Ne Hollywood studio, ne Casey Neistat-style production. Tu pasakai vieną tikrą istoriją per 8 minutes.

Equestrian niche'e (50K LT/PL/DACH equestrian-related YouTube searches/mėn.), competition mažai. „Stable management software" search'as duoda <10 results, beveik visi US-built. Mūsų video — pirmas EU founder POV apie šitą topic. SEO-wise, lock'inam #1 spot per 6–12 mėn.

I run Trakų Jojimo Klubas — a riding club about 28 kilometers from Vilnius, in Lithuania. We have 18 horses, three trainers including me, and around 40 active clients. We do 110 lessons in a typical month.

This is a mid-sized European riding operation. Big enough that informal tools start breaking. Small enough that enterprise software is wildly overkill.

For about three years, we ran TJK on the same operational stack as most stables our size in Europe — a WhatsApp group with the trainers, an Excel spreadsheet I updated on Sundays, a paper diary in the office, hand-written invoicing, and my own memory for which horse needed a rest week.

### [1:30–3:00] THE FAILURE — Caspar story

**Visual:** Soft cut to one of the horses (Caspar specifically if visible — or any horse close-up). Then back to talking head.

**Spoken:**

```
[1:30] — Tight shot of horse

We have a horse named Caspar. Twelve years old, gentle disposition, very popular with our intermediate riders. Last February, Caspar pulled out for two weeks of recovery — soft tissue strain, nothing dramatic, but enough to need rest.

[1:55] — Cut back to medium shot, you in tack room

When we looked back at the previous three weeks of his schedule — across all my mental notes, the WhatsApp messages, the Excel sheet, the paper diary — we'd worked Caspar about 30% more than I had estimated.

[2:15] — Slight head shake, eyes down then back to camera

I had been wrong. Confidently wrong. About a horse I love and a yard I run with care. Because the information was scattered across five different tools, and no single screen would have shown me Caspar was approaching his limit.

[2:35] — Pause

Two weeks of his lessons gone. Some of his career condition gone. About fifteen hundred euros in lost lesson revenue gone. And the trust signal to my team — that I was going to be the one tracking this carefully — slightly damaged.

[2:50] — Look directly at camera

I started building Longrein the next month.
```

### [3:00–5:00] THE RESEARCH — why nothing existed

**Visual:** Tablet showing Longrein dashboard, then cut to you scrolling through competitor websites on a laptop. B-roll of various stable operations shots.

**Spoken:**

```
[3:00] — Medium shot, you holding tablet showing Longrein

I looked at every stable management software on the market before deciding to build my own. Equine Genie. Stablebuzz. Barnmanager. EquineOffice. Hipocamp. None of them fit.

[3:25] — Cut to you scrolling on laptop

Three reasons.

[3:35] — Look at camera

One: every single one was American. Their pricing was in dollars. Their templates assumed US tax categories. Their support team was eight hours behind my time zone. Their approach to GDPR was an afterthought. None had SEPA Direct Debit, which is how Lithuanian and Polish and German stable owners actually want to bill their boarding clients.

[4:00] — Pause

Two: every single one buried welfare data under a „Reports" tab. Welfare was treated like an analytics feature you visit once a quarter. For me, welfare is the daily question: which horse is approaching cap, which one needs a rest week. That belongs at the top of the dashboard, before the calendar, before everything else.

[4:30] — Cut to you in stall area, closer with camera

And three: none of them were built by someone who actually runs a stable. They were built by software companies that hired equestrian consultants. The product reflected that — features designed for marketing demos, not for the muddy boots reality.

[4:50] — Beat

So I started writing what I needed.
```

## Post-production plan

### Editing in iMovie (or Mac equivalent)

1. Import all footage
2. Build skeleton timeline — drop in talking-head segments first per script
3. Layer B-roll on top of talking-head where script indicates „Cut to..."
4. Add lower-third title overlay (Cream + Paddock Source Serif)
5. Add end title card (Cream background, brand wordmark + URL)
6. Background music — instrumental, soft. Royalty-free from YouTube Audio Library OR Epidemic Sound (€15/mo subscription).
7. Audio leveling — talking-head -12dB, music -28dB

**Editing time:** 4–6 hours for first-time iMovie user. ~2 hours if you've used it before.

### Captions

CRITICAL — accessibility + SEO + YouTube watch-time.

1. Upload to YouTube
2. YouTube auto-generates captions
3. Manually review each line (~30 min of video × 2 review pace = 60 min)
4. Fix mispronunciations especially „Trakų", „Lithuania" pronunciations + technical terms
5. Save

## Decision points

### Before filming

- [ ] Confirm filming date in May (target May 12–14, after dogfood week begins)
- [ ] Buy/borrow lavalier mic if filming earlier than that
- [ ] Test iPhone audio with sample 30-sec recording before full filming day
- [ ] Print script — REHEARSE 3× before filming (NE memorize verbatim — get rhythm)

### During filming

- [ ] Shoot B-roll FIRST (warm up to camera presence)
- [ ] Talking-head AFTER B-roll (you'll be more comfortable)
- [ ] 3 takes of each segment minimum — pick best in editing
- [ ] If a segment isn't clicking — change location, NOT script. Sometimes new room = new energy.

### Before publishing

- [ ] Watch full video privately, then with one trusted person (NOT family — family is too kind)
- [ ] If view count after 7 days is <50 — diagnose: is it title (low CTR), thumbnail (low CTR), or content (low retention)?
- [ ] If retention is <30% — first 60 seconds problem. Re-cut, re-publish under same URL.

## Honest assessment (mano nuomonė)

YouTube #1 video yra arguably tavo aukščiausio leverage'o vienkartinis content investment'as per pirmus 12 mėn. Štai kodėl:

1. **Evergreen.** IG post'as gauna reach'ą per pirmas 24 val ir miršta. YouTube video — gauna reach'ą per 12+ mėn., vis stiprėja per SEO discovery.

2. **Trust signal'as.** Per LinkedIn jie mato post'us. Per IG mato grynai vizualus content'as. YouTube — žmogus mato tave kalbant 8 minutes. Trust'as išauga 10x.

3. **SEO real estate.** „Stable management software" search'as DACH'oje rodo <10 results, beveik visi US-built. Mūsų video gali lock'inti #1 spot per 6 mėn.

4. **Compounding.** Kai pirmas video gerai veikia, antras + trečias gauna 3x boost'ą iš YouTube algorithm'o (channel signal'as).

Bet — yra rizika. Filming + editing yra 2 days of work. Jei delegate'ini, ~€500–€1,000 fee for freelance editor. Jei pati daryai — ~12 valandų laiko sumitiniausiai.

Šitas time NEGINASI nuo Sav. 2 sprint'o (mobile dogfood). Reiks padalinti tarp dogfood'o + content filming. Dogfood'as svarbesnis. Filming'as moderavame jei dogfood'as nesibaigia.

**Mano formali rekomendacija:** filming May 14 (po 2 dienų dogfood'o). Editing weekend May 16–17. Publish May 23 (LAUNCH DAY). Galima.

Jei netinkama — push į Sav. 4 (May 26–28 filming, May 31 publish). Bet tu prarasi sinchronizacijos su LAUNCH DAY visibility.

Šito sprendimo cost'as: ~12 val tavo laiko. Tikėtina value 12 mėn.: priklausomai nuo execution, anywhere from 2K iki 20K views = stable owner discovery + brand authority.
