The five-tool problem

Right now your stable is run on five tools that do not talk to each other.

Excel for the schedule. Messaging apps for last-minute changes. A paper diary in the office. A separate group thread for the trainers. A printed invoice book at month-end.

Each one works on its own. Together they leak hours, money, and — sometimes — a tired horse who goes out for one ride too many.

The cost shows up in three places: four to eight hours of admin every week, €100–300 of unbilled lessons every month, and one preventable welfare incident a year that you don't see coming until the horse is already off work.

Longrein replaces all five with one screen.
Three things on one screen

The only stable tool that puts horse welfare on the same screen as the schedule and the invoice.

01 — Workload

See which horse worked too much this week. Before anyone else does.

Every horse has a workload heatmap and a weekly cap you set. The dashboard shows you who's near cap, who's over, who's been resting too long — at a glance, before you book the next lesson. No other stable software puts this on the front page. We did, because it's the question that matters most at 5am.

02 — Schedule

Lessons, livery, vet, farrier — one calendar, no double-bookings.

Drag-and-drop calendar across week and day views. Owners see everything; trainers see their lessons; clients see their own. Print this week's schedule and tape it to the tack room wall in two clicks. The database physically prevents a horse or trainer from being booked into two lessons at once. Not as a check the app runs — as a guarantee the schema enforces.

03 — Payments

One ledger for lessons, packs, and boarding — and a printable invoice in two clicks.

Lesson packages with remaining-balance tracking. Recurring boarding charges generated in one click for every horse. Per-client account summaries that show exactly what's outstanding. Payments are recorded by you when they arrive — cash, transfer, card. Self-pay through the client portal is on the roadmap for public launch.

Five questions

The things stable owners ask before they sign anything.

When does Longrein launch?

Founding Members onboard now through summer 2026 (10 seats). Public beta opens autumn 2026. Waitlist members get access before public launch.

Is my data safe?

Yes. EU-hosted (Frankfurt). GDPR-compliant from day one. One click to export everything as a CSV. One click to delete everything if you ever leave. Your data is yours.

Will it work on my phone?

Yes — that is where most of it is designed for. Trainers check in lessons in the arena from the phone. Clients see schedules and pay invoices on theirs. The desktop is the back office; the phone is the workplace.

What if I run a small backyard yard with three horses?

Longrein is built for stables with 15–40 horses. If yours is smaller, the Starter plan still works — but the product is most useful once you have at least one trainer who is not you.

Can I see it before I commit?

Yes. Founding Member applicants get a 45-minute onboarding call with the founder before signing anything. Waitlist members get a 90-second tour by email when public beta opens, plus a free 14-day trial — no card.
